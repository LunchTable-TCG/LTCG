## Overview

LunchTable TCG features three main card types, each serving a unique strategic purpose on the battlefield.

## Creatures

Creatures are the backbone of your army. They attack opponents and defend your Life Points.

### Creature Stats

| Stat | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Cost** | Mana required to summon |
| **ATK** | Attack power - damage dealt in combat |
| **HP** | Health points - damage creature can take |
| **Archetype** | Faction affinity (Infernal Dragons, Necro Empire, etc.) |

### Summoning Creatures

1. Pay the mana cost during Main Phase
2. Place creature in an empty Creature Zone
3. Creature has **summoning sickness** - cannot attack this turn

<Aside type="tip">
  Plan your summons! You can only have 4 creatures on the field at once.
</Aside>

### Creature Abilities

Creatures can have various ability types:

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Triggered">
    Activates automatically when a condition is met.

    **Example:** "When this creature enters the battlefield, draw 1 card."
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Activated">
    Pay a cost to activate manually.

    **Example:** "Tap, pay 2 mana: Deal 3 damage to target creature."
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Continuous">
    Always active while the creature is on the field.

    **Example:** "Your other Ember creatures get +1 ATK."
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Static">
    Modifies game rules.

    **Example:** "Opponents cannot target this creature with spells."
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Spells

Spells are one-time effects that go to the graveyard after use.

### Casting Spells

1. Pay the mana cost during Main Phase
2. Declare targets (if any)
3. Resolve the effect
4. Spell goes to graveyard

### Spell Categories

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Damage** | Deal direct damage to creatures or players |
| **Healing** | Restore Life Points or HP |
| **Buff** | Boost creature stats temporarily |
| **Debuff** | Weaken enemy creatures |
| **Draw** | Draw additional cards |
| **Removal** | Destroy or exile cards |

<Aside type="note">
  **Quick-Play Spells** can be cast during your opponent's turn in response to their actions!
</Aside>

## Traps

Traps are face-down cards that activate when triggered by specific conditions.

### Setting Traps

1. Set trap face-down during Main Phase (costs no mana)
2. Trap cannot activate the turn it is set
3. When trigger condition is met, you may activate the trap
4. Trap goes to graveyard after resolving

### Trap Timing

<Aside type="caution" title="Trap Timing">
  Traps can be activated:
  - During opponent's turn
  - In response to attacks
  - In response to spells
  - In response to abilities

  They **cannot** activate the turn they are set!
</Aside>

### Common Trap Types

| Type | Trigger | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Counter** | When opponent casts a spell | Negate the spell |
| **Defensive** | When opponent attacks | Reduce or negate damage |
| **Offensive** | When you attack | Boost your attack |
| **Removal** | When creature enters | Destroy the creature |
