# LTCG Card Types

## Monster Cards

Monster cards are the primary battle units in LTCG. They attack, defend, and often have special effects.

### Monster Card Anatomy

- **Card Name**: Unique identifier
- **Attribute**: LIGHT, DARK, FIRE, WATER, EARTH, WIND, DIVINE
- **Level**: Stars (1-12) indicating power and tribute requirements
- **Type**: Dragon, Warrior, Spellcaster, Beast, etc.
- **ATK (Attack Points)**: Offensive power
- **DEF (Defense Points)**: Defensive power
- **Effect Text**: Special abilities (if any)

### Monster Categories

#### Normal Monsters
- No effects
- Pure ATK/DEF stats
- Yellow/beige card border
- Often used as tribute fodder or beaters

#### Effect Monsters
- Have special abilities
- Orange card border
- Most common monster type
- Effects vary widely

#### Ritual Monsters
- Require ritual spell to summon
- Blue card border
- Usually powerful
- Go in Main Deck

#### Fusion Monsters
- Summoned by fusing specific monsters
- Purple card border
- Stored in Extra Deck
- Require Fusion Spell or effect

#### Synchro Monsters
- Summoned using Tuner + non-Tuner monsters
- White card border
- Level equals sum of materials
- Stored in Extra Deck

#### XYZ Monsters
- Summoned by overlaying monsters of same level
- Black card border
- Use materials as "Overlay Units"
- Stored in Extra Deck

#### Link Monsters
- Summoned using monsters as Link Material
- Blue/cyan card border
- No DEF stat
- Link Arrows point to zones
- Stored in Extra Deck

### Monster Effect Types

#### Ignition Effects
- Activated manually during your Main Phase
- Notation: "You can [action]"
- Turn player priority

#### Trigger Effects
- Activate automatically when condition is met
- Notation: "When/If [condition], [effect]"
- Mandatory or optional

#### Quick Effects
- Can activate during either player's turn
- Notation: "During either player's turn, you can [action]"
- Spell Speed 2

#### Continuous Effects
- Always active while face-up
- Notation: "All [type] monsters gain..."
- No activation, cannot be chained

#### Flip Effects
- Activate when flipped face-up
- Notation: "FLIP: [effect]"
- Useful for defensive plays

### Monster Summoning Conditions

#### Cannot Be Normal Summoned/Set
- Must be Special Summoned
- Usually very powerful
- Specific summoning requirements

#### Special Summon Only
- Cannot be Normal Summoned
- Strict summoning procedure
- Example: Ritual, Fusion monsters

## Spell Cards

Spell cards provide various effects to support your strategy.

### Spell Card Types

#### Normal Spell
- Green card border
- Activate during your Main Phase
- One-time effect, then to Graveyard
- Most common spell type

#### Quick-Play Spell
- Green card with lightning bolt
- Can be activated during either player's turn
- Can be set face-down
- Spell Speed 2 (can chain to other effects)

#### Continuous Spell
- Green card with infinity symbol
- Remains on field after activation
- Effect persists while face-up
- Vulnerable to removal

#### Field Spell
- Green card with compass/gear icon
- Goes in Field Spell Zone
- Affects entire field
- Both players can use Field Spell Zone

#### Equip Spell
- Green card with cross/plus icon
- Attaches to a monster
- Grants bonuses or effects
- Destroyed when equipped monster leaves field

#### Ritual Spell
- Blue card
- Used to Ritual Summon monsters
- Requires tributing monsters
- Usually one-time use

### Spell Activation Timing

- **Your Main Phase**: Normal and Continuous Spells
- **Any Time**: Quick-Play Spells (if set for 1 turn)
- **Response Window**: Quick-Play can chain to opponent's actions

## Trap Cards

Trap cards are reactive effects that disrupt your opponent's strategy.

### Trap Card Types

#### Normal Trap
- Pink/magenta card border
- One-time effect
- Must be set for 1 turn before activation
- Goes to Graveyard after use

#### Continuous Trap
- Pink card with infinity symbol
- Remains on field after activation
- Persistent effect
- Must be set for 1 turn first

#### Counter Trap
- Pink card with curved arrow
- Spell Speed 3 (highest)
- Can negate other cards
- Only Counter Traps can chain to Counter Traps

### Trap Activation Rules

- Must be set face-down first
- Cannot activate same turn it was set (unless card says otherwise)
- Can activate during opponent's turn
- Spell Speed 2 (Normal and Continuous) or 3 (Counter)

### Common Trap Strategies

#### Disruption Traps
- Destroy opponent's monsters or cards
- Example: "Destroy 1 monster"

#### Negation Traps
- Counter opponent's activations
- Prevent effects from resolving

#### Protection Traps
- Shield your cards from destruction
- Prevent targeting

#### Stall Traps
- Delay opponent's plays
- Buy time to set up

## Card Effect Terminology

### Targeting
- "Target 1 monster..." = Targeting effect
- Chosen at activation
- Cannot target untargetable cards

### Destruction
- "Destroy..." = Send to Graveyard via effect
- Different from battle destruction
- Can be prevented by protection

### Negation
- "Negate the activation..."
- Prevents effect from resolving
- Card may or may not be destroyed

### Banish
- "Banish..." = Remove from play
- Sent to Banished Zone
- Harder to recover than Graveyard

### Send
- "Send to the Graveyard..."
- Not destruction (bypasses some protections)
- Direct movement

### Discard
- "Discard..." = Send from hand to Graveyard
- Usually a cost
- Triggers "when discarded" effects

### Tribute
- "Tribute..." = Send to Graveyard for summoning
- Not destruction
- Required for high-level summons

### Detach
- "Detach..." = Remove XYZ material
- Not sent to Graveyard (usually)
- Cost for XYZ effects

## Card Rarity and Power

### Rarity Levels
- **Common (C)**: Basic cards, widely available
- **Rare (R)**: Uncommon, useful effects
- **Super Rare (SR)**: Powerful cards, shiny
- **Ultra Rare (UR)**: Very powerful, holographic
- **Secret Rare**: Extremely rare, premium effects

### Power Creep
- Newer cards tend to be stronger
- Older archetypes get support
- Meta shifts with new releases

## Archetype Cards

### Archetype Definition
- Cards with shared name theme
- Example: "Blue-Eyes", "Dark Magician", "Elemental HERO"
- Support each other with synergistic effects

### Archetype Support
- Cards that specifically help archetype
- "If you control a [archetype] monster..."
- Deck-building around archetypes

### Generic vs. Archetype-Specific
- Generic: Works in any deck
- Archetype-specific: Only works with specific cards
- Balance between consistency and power
